Breeding Birds in Europe

Every spring birds work hard to build their nests, perhaps in your garden like the robin or on your roofs like the Eurasian Oystercatcher has started to do in Amsterdam. In the dunes, the forests or the tundras. All over Europe birds make their nests. Here the dataset of the EBCC Atlas of the European Breeding Birds (data from 1972-1995) has been visualised to show what the most common genuses were per country species-wise. Which countries score high on the number of breeding species, which countries made a lot of observations. If you are curious in which countries a certain species is present you can select that species in the dropdown menu.





Two different views can be selected for the map: total number of species observed in a country or the total number of observations. The species map shows that especially Russia has a high number of breeding bird species. Russia is the only country with more than 300 species. However the number of observations made in a country differs greatly. It ranges from 47 in Kazachstan to 29 468 observations in France. Therefore the number of observations in these visualisations does not say much about the abundance of the species, only about the presence/absence of a species in a country. In order to explore what type of birds occur much in a country, the top ten genuses with the most species can be viewed by clicking a country. If a bar from a genus is clicked the specific species are shown in a bargraph. More information about the genus or species can be obtained through the link to the wikipedia site on hovering above a bar.

By selecting a bird species in the dropdownmenu below, the countries where a specific bird breeds can be viewed. In the map it is clearly visible that some species only breed in the northern areas like the Siberian Jay, whereas other species prefer the mediterranean climate better.
