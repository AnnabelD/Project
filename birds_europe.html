Breeding Birds in Europe

Every spring birds work hard to build their nests, perhaps in your garden like the robin or on your roofs like the Eurasian Oystercatcher has started to do in Amsterdam. In the dunes, the forests or the tundras. All over Europe birds make their nests. Here the dataset of the EBCC Atlas of the European Breeding Birds (data from 1972-1995) has been visualised to show what the most common genuses were per country species-wise. Which countries score high on the number of breeding species, which countries made a lot of observations. If you are curious in which countries a certain species is present you can select that species in the dropdown menu.




Several different views can be selected for the map: total number of species observed in a country, total number of observations or the total number of species divided by the area of the country. The species map shows that there is no great difference between countries concerning the biodiversity of breeding birds. Only the countries at the edges of europe show less diverse observations. However the total number of observations within these countries was rather low.
